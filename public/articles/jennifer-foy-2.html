ennifer Carroll Foy

Jennifer Foy has dedicated her life to public service. She is a public defender and represents some of the Commonwealth’s most vulnerable citizens including adolescents, the indigent and the mentally ill. In addition, she is an adjunct professor at Northern Virginia Community College. She has also served as a magistrate judge in Richmond. Prior to that, she practiced law as a litigation associate.

Jennifer was born and raised in Petersburg, VA. Upon graduating from Petersburg High School, she earned a full scholarship to Virginia Military Institute and became one of the first females to graduate from the historically all-male college. She then earned a Master’s degree with honors from Virginia State University. She taught briefly before earning a law degree from Thomas Jefferson Law School.

To date, one of her proudest contributions to the community is the creation of the Foundation for Foster and Orphan Children. The organization’s mission is to help build a better future for foster and orphan children in Virginia and beyond. She has been a foster parent for many years. She lives in Woodbridge, Virginia with her husband, Jeffrey Foy.

Her work on behalf of the citizens of Virginia gives her a clearunderstanding of the needs of the Commonwealth, especially of District 2 where she lives.
